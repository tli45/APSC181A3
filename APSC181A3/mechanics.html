How Does the Ollie Work?

Understand the physics and motion behind this iconic move.

Dynamics Breakdown

Ollie consists of three different motion systems: the jumping motion of the rider and the rotating motion of the board. In other words, ollie is simply the combined motion of jumping and kicking the board.

The jumping motion system consists only of the rider, who jumps on the board. Simply put, what a rider does in an ollie is simply jumping on a piece of moving board. A detailed analysis showed that the rider exerts the jumping force onto the board, and the board propagates this force to the ground, resulting in a reaction force exerted on the rider. This causes the rider (mass body) to move upwards with an initial force approximately similar to the jumping force in magnitude.

The rotating motion of the board was caused by the kicking force (jumping force); the wheels act as rotating axis and the support, thus end up in a moment at the axis and lift the front end of the board up. This motion happens rapidly, which the inertial property causes the board to do deceleration motion. The mass center locates at the middle of the board which do similar motion will end up in a straight line constant velocity motion flying in the tangent direction of the last point of the rotating curve. When the board hits the front foot of the rider, the board would result in another moment causing the rear-part of the board to do another rotating motion towards the back foot.

Lastly, when the back foot hits the board, the rider and the board are at its maximum height.
